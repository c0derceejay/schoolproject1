About Me

Hello, my name is Chris Jackson. I am 30 years old and a Freelance Web Developer pursuing my Bachelors degree in Information Technology. I am a Father to a 2 year old daughter and she is my world, always keeping me on my toes and in tune with the latest Disney movie or character. I have been serving Active Duty in the US Army for the past 11 years now, and have decided to pursue my dreams as a Developer after my current contract ends. For now I have been spending my time learning as much as I can about Web Development and more so Python programming. My web development experience comes from the last 2 years of completing online bootcamps and following along Youtube tutorials and I have built a solid foundation with those resources.

"Look up at the stars and not down at your feet. Try to make sense of what you see, and wonder about what makes the universe exist. Be curious. - Stephen Hawking"

I am very excited to be apart of this course with a focus on Web Development because I feel that this is a great opportunity to learn a skill that can be used for life, creating freelance opportunity and consistent income as long as you are willing to learn the most up to date design principles and tricks to stand out amongst the competition! In todays world, having a website to showcase what it is that you do or what your business is for, is very important. This will allow users all over the world to get direct access to your information and services whether at home on a computer or while youre traveling with a mobile phone or tablet.

I believe having a solid portfolio is required as a Web Developer who wishes to seek potential clients. If you have no work to showcase so that people can see your skills and what you are capable of doing, then finding work will be even harder. With online resources like Github Pages or even Wordpress, it has become alot easier for developers to showcase there work without having to put up alot of money to publish it. Of course if you wish to look more professional and have a custom domain, then having a website publish through a hosting service with a paid domain is definitely the route to go.
